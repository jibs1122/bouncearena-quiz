When it comes to high-end spring trampolines in Australia, two models stand out: the JumpFlex Hero and the Vuly Ultra 2 Pro. Both represent the pinnacle of their respective brands' spring trampoline offerings.

These spring-loaded marvels aren't just about fun – they're engineering feats designed to provide safe, exhilarating experiences for kids and adults alike.

Let's dive into a detailed comparison to help you make an informed decision and choose the right option for your family.

## Size and Capacity

While both trampolines are similar in overall size, there are some notable differences:

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  | HERO 10ft | Ultra 2 Pro (M) | HERO 12ft | Ultra 2 Pro (L) | HERO 14ft | Ultra 2 Pro (XL) | HERO 15ft |
| Frame Width (at widest point) | 350cm | 352cm | 410cm | 412cm | 460cm | 474cm | 480cm |
| Floor to Mat Height | 89cm | 91cm | 89cm | 91cm | 89cm | 91cm | 89cm |

| Safety Net Height | 170cm | 191cm | 170cm | 191cm | 170cm | 191cm | 170cm |
| Mat Diameter | 250cm | 255cm | 320cm | 315cm | 370cm | 375cm | 400cm |

JumpFlex offers a wider range of sizes, including a 15ft option, while Vuly's largest model is the XL (comparable to JumpFlex's 14ft). The Vuly models are also consistently taller, with an 18cm greater overall height and a 21cm taller safety net.

![](/images/posts/vuly-ultra-2-pro.png)

As we can see from the table, Vuly prioritises taller safety nets in their range, while Jumpflex offers more size options with consistent dimensions, potentially catering to a wider variety of space requirements.

The JumpFlex Hero boasts an impressive weight capacity of 250kg, while Vuly hasn't yet specified the capacity for the Ultra 2 Pro. JumpFlex also provides more detailed size and weight specifications, noting an assembled weight of 105kg.

## Spring Technology and Bounce

Both trampolines use spring technology, but with different approaches:

- JumpFlex Hero: 80 high-tensile extension springs, each 180mm long
- Vuly Ultra 2 Pro: Dual-coil springs with dual frames for added stability

## Safety Features

Safety is a priority for both brands:

- Both feature curved safety net poles and superfine mesh
- JumpFlex uses EndlessWeave™ technology for extra durability in their mesh
- Vuly offers a self-closing entry, while JumpFlex uses a zip closure
- JumpFlex highlights their SafeSeal™ technology, which they describe as "ensuring the net is securely clipped to the jumping mat, preventing accidents"

JumpFlex emphasizes their "360-degree protection" and notes that their curved poles provide "more space between poles and jumping mat" for added safety.

Vuly has introduced new flexible net poles across their range. As they state, "This means they can take extreme force without getting damaged or bent!"

## Frame and Durability

Both trampolines are built to last:

- Both use galvanised and powder-coated steel frames
- JumpFlex boasts a 6-layer anti-rust protection
- Vuly emphasizes their superior rust protection, stating their frames are "powder-coated for superior rust protection"

## Accessories and Add-ons

Both brands offer a range of accessories to enhance the trampoline experience:

- Both offer shade covers, basketball hoops, and anchor kits
- JumpFlex includes a ladder and offers a unique slide accessory
- Vuly offers a tent wall accessory, which JumpFlex doesn't have

Vuly has also introduced a new feature: "For the first time ever, BOTH a Basketball Hoop and Shade Cover can be used at the same time."

![](/images/posts/vuly-shade-cover-and-basketball.jpg)

## Price and Warranty

- At the time of writing, both trampolines are priced at $1,199 AUD for the large (12ft) models
- Both also offer identical warranty terms: 10 years for the frame, 5 years for springs and mat, and 1 year for safety net and pads

## Company Background

- JumpFlex is from New Zealand, while Vuly is an Australian company
- Both manufacture their trampolines in China

## Conclusion

Both the JumpFlex Hero and Vuly Ultra 2 Pro offer premium features at the same price point. They're evenly matched in many areas, but each has its unique strengths:

### JumpFlex Hero

- More size options, including a 15ft model
- Higher specified weight capacity
- Included ladder and option of unique slide accessory

### Vuly Ultra 2 Pro

- Taller safety net and overall height
- Innovative dual-coil spring system
- Ability to use basketball hoop and shade cover together
- Option of unique tent wall accessory

Your choice may come down to specific features that matter most to your family, such as mat size, safety net height, or available accessories. Both trampolines represent the high end of spring technology and should provide years of bouncing fun for Australian families.

Remember to consider factors like your available space, the age and number of users, and which specific features align best with your family's needs. Whichever you choose, both the JumpFlex Hero and Vuly Ultra 2 Pro promise to deliver a premium trampoline experience.
