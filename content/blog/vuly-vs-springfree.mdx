In this article, we explore these aspects in-depth to help you make an informed decision tailored to your specific needs, whether that's prioritising safety, budget, or durability. Read on to discover how each brand stands up to rigorous everyday use and find out which trampoline is the best fit for your backyard.

## Build Quality and Durability

### Vuly Thunder Pro

The Vuly Thunder Pro is constructed with a galvanised, powder-coated steel frame, enhancing its durability and resistance to rust. Our expert emphasises, "It’s solid and strong in construction," noting that its weight significantly exceeds that of standard models, providing extra stability. He comments "this trampoline does not blow away in any wind."

Despite its robustness, one downside is its potential to sink into soft ground due to its weight and small feet, making it less ideal for grassy areas and better suited for harder surfaces.

### Vuly Thunder

The Thunder shares the galvanised steel construction of the Pro but lacks the powder coating, which may affect its longevity and rust resistance slightly. Like the Pro, it is designed for durability but has the same issues with the feet sinking into softer surfaces, which can affect stability and make relocation for lawn care challenging.

Both Vuly models feature a self-closing entry, which we appreciate for ease of use and for preventing the common issue of broken zippers.

![](/images/posts/vuly-thunder-springs.jpg)

### Springfree

Springfree trampolines also feature a galvanised, powder-coated steel frame, known for its longevity. Our expert regards this as "the longest-lasting trampoline frame on the market," praising its ability to endure the elements. He also praises the “extra durable net and mat”, commenting that there are generally no parts that need replacing from normal use.

In terms of potential issues, he does note that the zip can eventually break if care is not taken. He also adds that the cosmetic ribbon bordering the mat can wear out in the doorway area.

## Safety Features

### Vuly Thunder Pro

This model is equipped with curved safety net poles and superfine mesh netting that enhances jumper safety. Our expert appreciates its design: "The mat sealing neatly with the net ensures the safety of your child."

He also highlights the safety net height, stating "the Vuly Thunder Pro has high nets to keep everyone safe." In fact, at 190 cm, this trampoline has the highest safety net of any model we’ve looked at.

The use of Vuly’s innovative leaf springs means there are no coil springs for small hands to get trapped in and also means the frame is located away from the jumping area, and outside the safety net, further reducing the risk of injury.

The full-length frame combined with the skirt means that there is no underside access on the Thunder Pro, however, keep in mind that this also makes it almost impossible to mow under.

The Thunder Pro complies with the Australian trampoline standard AS 4989:2015, ensuring it meets rigorous safety benchmarks.

![](/images/posts/vuly-thunder-pro-3.jpg)

### Vuly Thunder

The Thunder also utilises leaf springs to avoid the risks of coil springs and features a design that keeps structural elements away from jumpers. It has a slightly lower safety net compared to the Thunder Pro, but adheres to both Australian and European safety standards.

As with the Thunder Pro, our expert appreciates how the net attaches directly to the mat and a skirt covers the leaf springs, further reducing the chance of injury. In his view, “this trampoline is one of the safest on the market."

### Springfree

Renowned for safety, Springfree uses fibreglass rods instead of springs, which removes the risks of pinching and injuries associated with springs. "No springs to pinch you and a strong and flexible net to keep everyone safe," notes our expert.

It also features curved safety net poles and superfine mesh, providing a very secure bouncing space. Like the Vuly models, this trampoline meets the Australian Trampoline Standard AS 4989:2015.

This brand is often considered the safest on the market, making it a top choice for family backyards. In fact, our expert considers it to be “the safest and longest-lasting trampoline in Australian backyards”.

![](/images/posts/springfree-trampoline-3.png)

## Bounce and Performance

While springless trampoline designs are often considered inferior in terms of bounce quality, all of these models deliver satisfactory bounce, especially for younger kids.

### Vuly Thunder Pro

The leaf spring system of the Thunder Pro adjusts to different weights, providing a consistent and enjoyable bounce. However, "like most trampolines, it depends on the size of the person jumping on it," our expert notes.

### Vuly Thunder

Similar to the Pro, the Thunder offers a decent bounce. However, performance might not be as optimal for lighter users. Despite this, it remains a strong performer in the family trampoline category, providing a safe and enjoyable experience.

### Springfree

Springfree trampolines are praised for their bounce quality despite lacking traditional springs. They can support a significant amount of weight, making them versatile for families of various sizes and weights.

Due to the design, some people report that Springfree models twist a bit on the bounce. However, it only seems to be trampoline enthusiasts/gymnasts that take issue with this.

![](/images/posts/springfree-medium-round-trampoline-2.jpg)

## Ease of Assembly

In general, springless trampolines present a more difficult assembly process than traditional designs. Many people, therefore, opt to engage a professional assembly service for these models.

### Vuly Thunder Pro

The Thunder Pro presents some challenges in assembly. "According to customers, the assembly is quite difficult, compounded by vague instructions," reports our expert. The complex setup may necessitate professional assistance or at least a thorough viewing of [Vuly's online assembly videos](https://www.vulyplay.com/aff/100/?url=installation).

“The spring tool that comes with the trampoline can actually make assembly harder as it seems to position the end of the leaf spring away from the mat that you’re trying to unite with”, he explains.

![](/images/posts/vuly-thunder-pro-assembly.png)

### Vuly Thunder

Assembly of the Thunder is similarly challenging, with unclear instructions sometimes complicating the process. The design, while innovative, requires careful attention during setup.

### Springfree

While Springfree models are also complex to assemble, the clarity of instructions and supportive online tutorials help. "Stickers aid in aligning the net, mat, and frame correctly, simplifying the process," notes our expert.

Regardless, professional assembly is still recommended for those seeking a hassle-free setup.

## Accessories

While all models have a range of available accessories, none of them come standard with a ladder or anchor kit, which may be considered essential by some customers.

### Vuly Thunder and Thunder Pro

Both Vuly models support a range of accessories like shade covers, basketball hoops, and tent walls for added enjoyment and functionality.

### Springfree

The Springfree trampoline also supports a variety of accessories, including a shade cover and basketball hoop.

## Value for Money

### Vuly Thunder Pro

The Thunder Pro, priced between $2.4k and $3.6k, is among the most expensive models available. Despite the high cost, it offers advanced safety features and robust construction, which might justify the investment for those prioritising safety and durability. However, its high price is likely to deter some buyers.

### Vuly Thunder

The Thunder is priced over $2k for the Large model, making it a significant investment, though less than the Thunder Pro. We think it provides decent value with its safety features and innovative springless design, appealing to those seeking a safe and durable trampoline at a slightly more accessible price point.

### Springfree

Springfree's trampolines are expensive but offer excellent safety and durability, potentially providing the best long-term value. The high cost is balanced by an extensive warranty and a strong resale value.

## Customer Service and Warranty

### Vuly Thunder and Thunder Pro

Both models come with a strong warranty—10 years on the frame and 5 years on the springs and mat. However, customer service feedback is mixed, with some customers reporting positive interactions and others facing challenges, especially when dealing with assembly or maintenance issues.

### Springfree

Springfree offers an extensive 10-year warranty across all major components and generally receives more consistent positive feedback regarding customer service.

## Conclusion

Both Vuly and Springfree offer exceptional products that stand out for their safety and quality. While both brands share similar features like galvanised steel frames and compliance with Australian safety standards, they each bring unique advantages to the table.

When deciding between Vuly and Springfree, factors such as budget, specific safety features, durability expectations, and ease of assembly should be considered. Although both brands command a higher price point, they justify this with their advanced safety features, long-lasting materials, and strong warranties.

Vuly Thunder trampolines are known for their robust construction and innovative safety features such as the leaf spring system, which enhances safety by eliminating coil springs. They also offer a truly vertical bounce.

On the other hand, Springfree trampolines arguably boast superior durability. Similarly, they feature a safety design that reduces the risk of injury with springless fibreglass rods.

No matter which brand you choose, both Vuly and Springfree offer a safe and enjoyable bouncing experience that your family can cherish for many years.
