According to a recent report by [CHOICE](https://www.choice.com.au/babies-and-kids/children-and-safety/toys-and-safety-at-play/buying-guides/trampolines), Australia's leading consumer advocacy group, most trampolines on the market fail to meet safety standards.

This may be alarming news for parents looking to add some backyard fun for their kids. But don't let it deflate your spirits - with the right knowledge, you can still find a safe option for your family.

CHOICE's findings underscore the importance of careful research when shopping for a trampoline. While these bouncy marvels can provide hours of active outdoor entertainment, they're also a common source of injuries.

Let's jump into what they say about choosing a safe trampoline and using it properly.

### CHOICE's Key Findings

1. **Safety Standard Failures:** The majority of tested trampolines did not meet the Australian safety standard AS 4989:2015.
2. **Critical Safety Features:** CHOICE emphasises the importance of proper safety padding, secure enclosures, and springless designs in mitigating risks.
3. **Installation and Maintenance:** Proper setup and regular checks are crucial for ongoing safety.
4. **Usage Guidelines:** Supervision and enforcing rules like 'one jumper at a time' are essential for safe use.
5. **Injury Statistics:** Trampolines are the second leading cause of play equipment injuries in Australia, with children aged 5-9 most at risk.
6. **Price Not Indicative of Safety:** Both cheap and expensive models failed various safety tests.
7. **Push for Mandatory Standards:** CHOICE advocates for making the current voluntary standard mandatory.

## Understanding the Australian Safety Standard

The Australian safety standard AS 4989:2015 for trampolines covers various aspects of design and construction. Key requirements include:

- Padding that fully covers the frame and springs
- No gaps that could trap limbs
- A secure enclosure to prevent falls
- Clear user instructions and safety warnings

It's worth noting that compliance with this standard is currently voluntary. However, responsible manufacturers strive to meet or exceed these guidelines to ensure user safety.

## Top Safety Features to Look For

When shopping for a trampoline, keep an eye out for these safety features:

- Springless design: Models using flexible composite rods instead of metal springs
- Net enclosures: Sturdy, well-attached nets to prevent falls
- Adequate padding: Thick, securely attached padding covering all hard surfaces
- UV-resistant materials: To withstand our harsh sun
- Weather-resistant frame: Galvanised or powder-coated steel to resist rust and corrosion
- Safety pad retention system: To keep pads securely in place
- Soft-edge mat: Reduces impact on joints
- No-gap design: Eliminates spaces where limbs could get trapped
- Rust-resistant springs: If not using a springless design
- Removable ladder: To control access when not in use

## Safe Trampoline Use: Rules for the Whole Family

To minimise risks and maximise fun, establish and enforce these rules:

- One jumper at a time
- No somersaults or risky stunts
- Always jump in the centre of the mat
- Remove jewellery and empty pockets before jumping
- No jumping onto or off the trampoline
- Adult supervision required at all times
- No use when wet or in windy conditions

## Trampoline Alternatives for Active Backyards

If you're not comfortable with a traditional trampoline, consider these alternatives:

- In-ground trampolines: Reduced fall heights and blend into landscaping
- Rebounding mini-trampolines: Great for indoor use and gentle on joints
- Natural play areas: Create a backyard circuit with balance beams, logs, and climbing structures

## Trampoline Maintenance

Regular maintenance is crucial for both [trampoline safety](https://www.reddit.com/r/TrampolineOwnersAu/comments/1fo12eh/safest_trampolines_in_australia_choice_report/) and longevity:

- Inspect the mat and springs for wear after heavy rains or extreme heat
- Keep the jumping surface clean of leaves and debris
- Trim nearby plants regularly to prevent interference with the structure
- Consider a cover to protect the trampoline when not in use

By following these guidelines, you can ensure that your trampoline remains a safe and enjoyable addition to your garden for years to come.
