Picture this: it's a sunny afternoon, your kids are bursting with energy, and you want them to have a safe and enjoyable place to play. A trampoline seems like the perfect solution.

But as you dive into your research, you're faced with a dilemma: spring or springless? Each type promises to be the best option, but which one truly lives up to the hype?

This isn't just about a simple backyard toy. The right trampoline can provide years of entertainment, encourage physical activity, and even become a centrepiece for family gatherings.

So, let's explore the pros and cons of springless and spring trampolines to help you make the right choice for your family.

## What is a Springless Trampoline?

A springless trampoline is a type of trampoline that eliminates the use of traditional metal springs.

Instead, it employs alternative mechanisms such as heavy-duty elastic straps, flexible composite rods, or leaf springs.

These components are designed to provide the same bouncing effect without the risks associated with metal springs.

### Springless Trampoline Brands

In Australia, some of the prominent brands manufacturing springless trampolines include:

- **Springfree** - fibreglass rods
- **Vuly** - leaf springs
- **Kahuna** - elastic straps
- **Lifespan Kids** - elastic straps

## Frame Design and Safety

One of the key differences in the design of springless trampolines is the placement of the frame.

Unlike traditional trampolines where the frame is near the bouncing surface, many springless models often have the frame located well below.

![](/images/posts/vuly-thunder-1.jpg)

This design significantly reduces the risk of injuries as there's less chance for users to come into contact with the frame.

### No More Spring Injuries

Traditional trampolines have metal springs that can cause pinching injuries. Springless trampolines, on the other hand, replace these with safer alternatives. This means kids are less likely to land on springs or get their hands stuck in them.

## Cost Considerations

Springless trampolines are generally more expensive than their spring-based counterparts. This is due to the advanced materials and technology used in their construction. Brands like Springfree and Vuly invest heavily in research and development to ensure their trampolines are at the forefront of safety and performance.

Traditional spring trampolines tend to be more budget-friendly. If you're looking for a trampoline for short-term use or if your budget is limited, a spring-based model might be the better choice.

For those willing to invest more for enhanced safety and durability, springless trampolines offer excellent value. Although they come with a higher price tag, their advanced safety features and longer lifespan can justify the cost.

## Bounce Quality

The quality of the bounce is a crucial factor for many trampoline buyers. Traditional spring trampolines use metal springs to create a bounce. The tension in these springs provides a high and responsive bounce, which is ideal for gymnastics and acrobatics.

Springless trampolines, using mechanisms like composite rods or leaf springs, offer a different bounce experience. While they still provide a satisfactory bounce, they may not be up to the standard of high-end spring trampolines.

## Assembly

Assembling a springless trampoline can be a bit more challenging compared to traditional models. The process involves inserting rods or straps into their respective slots, which can require some physical strength. However, many brands provide detailed instructions and video tutorials to assist with the setup.

![](/images/posts/vuly-thunder-pro-assembly.png)

If you're not confident in assembling the trampoline yourself, many companies offer professional installation services. This can ensure that the trampoline is set up correctly and safely, giving you peace of mind.

## Who Should Choose a Spring Trampoline?

### Ideal for Budget-Conscious Buyers

If you're looking for an affordable option, a spring trampoline is a great choice. They are widely available and come in various sizes and designs to suit different needs.

### Great for Gymnastics and Tricks

Traditional spring trampolines provide a higher and more responsive bounce, making them ideal for activities like gymnastics and acrobatics. If your kids are into these sports, a spring trampoline might be more suitable.

## Who Should Choose a Springless Trampoline?

### Safety-Conscious Families

If safety is your top priority, a springless trampoline is the way to go. The innovative design reduces the risk of injuries, making it a safer option for young children.

## Conclusion

Choosing between a springless and a spring trampoline depends on your priorities. If safety and long-term value are your main concerns, a springless trampoline is the best option. However, if you're looking for a more affordable trampoline with a high bounce, a traditional spring model might be the way to go.
