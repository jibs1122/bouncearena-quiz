Choosing a backyard swing set means balancing durability, safety, space requirements, and how long your kids will actually use it. This guide compares four popular brands available in Australia: Vuly Max 2, Plum Play, Lifespan Kids, and Funky Monkey Bars.

Each brand takes a different approach. Vuly and Funky Monkey Bars use steel frames built for longevity. Plum Play and Lifespan Kids offer wooden sets that blend into garden settings, and these are the models I've focussed on for this comparison (they both also offer metal varieties).

## Vuly Max 2

![](/images/posts/vuly-swingset.png)

[**View at Vuly**](https://www.vulyplay.com/aff/100/?url=swingsets)

Vuly is a Brisbane-based company that designs steel swing sets with a focus on modularity. The Max 2 uses an A-frame design with double-galvanised, powder-coated steel and comes with an integrated shade cover.

### What parents should know

**No slide included.** This is purely a swing system. If you want swings plus a slide on the same frame, Vuly isn't the right choice—you'd need to look at Plum Play or Lifespan Kids instead.

**Modular and expandable.** You can start with a 2-bay frame and add a third bay later. Swings clip on and off, so you can swap between standard seats, nest swings, and other accessories as your kids grow.

**Assembly takes longer than advertised.** The official estimate is 2-3 hours, but parent feedback consistently suggests 4-8 hours is more realistic. Instructions are a common complaint—described as vague with poorly labelled bolts.

**Weight limit of 80kg per swing.** This limits adult participation compared to some competitors.

### Warranty

- 8 years on frame
- 2 years on swings
- 1 year on accessories

### Pros

- 8-year frame warranty—longer than timber alternatives, though shorter than Funky Monkey Bars
- Steel construction handles weather well without annual maintenance
- Large accessory ecosystem—easy to add new swings and attachments over time
- Strong resale market if you sell later

### Cons

- No slide option on the swing frame
- Higher price point, especially once you add accessories
- Assembly instructions need improvement
- Some reports of shade cover fabric fading in full sun
- Warranty reduced by 50% if installed within 1.6km of saltwater

## Plum Play

![](/images/posts/plum-play-swings.png)

[**View at Plum Play**](https://www.plumplay.com.au/collections/swings-and-slides?srsltid=AfmBOoq_t1gDsehK531kmcBWeH8IEt2jN1HZVK4dsNlMTrcneHrBihek)

Plum Play is a UK-based brand with over 35 years in the market. They offer wooden swing sets using FSC-certified European pine or Chinese fir, with many models combining swings, slides, and small platforms.

### What parents should know

**Good entry-level option.** Plum offers some of the most affordable complete swing sets with slides, making them accessible for families testing whether their kids will use outdoor play equipment.

**Compact footprints.** Sets like the Tamarin (two swings, platform, ladder, and 1.78m slide) fit well in standard suburban backyards.

**Brand reputation is mixed.** While swing set reviews are generally reasonable, Plum's trampolines rate poorly (1.4-2.1 out of 5 stars) with complaints about durability and spare parts availability. This doesn't mean their swing sets have the same issues, but it's worth being aware of if you're researching the brand online.

**Timber needs ongoing care.** You'll need to check for cracks, treat the wood annually, and watch for rust on metal hardware—especially in wetter climates or full sun.

### Warranty

- 12 months on most products
- 5 years for rot and insect damage on some premium wooden sets

### Pros

- FSC-certified sustainable timber
- Natural wooden look suits garden settings
- Many sets include slide, platform, and swings in one package
- Growing Swing model converts through three stages from baby to older child

### Cons

- Shorter warranty than steel alternatives
- Annual timber maintenance required
- Mixed brand reputation due to trampoline line issues
- Some reports of rust on metal parts and difficulty finding replacement components
- Quality control can be inconsistent—misaligned pre-drilled holes mentioned in some reviews

## Lifespan Kids

![](/images/posts/lifespankids.webp)

[**View at Lifespan Kids**](https://www.lifespankids.com.au/collections/swing-sets-play-centres)

Lifespan Kids is an Australian-owned company based in Melbourne, operating since 2008. They offer the widest range of the four brands, from budget metal frames through to large timber play centres with slides and climbing walls.

### What parents should know

**Only brand with budget metal options.** If you want something functional at the lowest price point, Lifespan's basic metal swing sets are the most affordable of the four brands.

**Showrooms in Melbourne and Sydney.** Unlike the other brands, you can see Lifespan products in person before buying at their Broadmeadows (VIC) and Wetherill Park (NSW) locations.

**Individual product reviews are good; brand-level reviews are not.** On ProductReview, specific swing sets like the Holt rate around 4.5/5. But the overall brand rating sits at 1.9/5 across 86 reviews, with recurring complaints about timber quality, missing parts, and frustrating warranty support. This disconnect suggests the products themselves are reasonable, but after-sales service can be problematic.

**Termite damage not covered.** The timber warranty excludes termite damage, which is worth noting if you're in Queensland or northern NSW where termites are common.

### Warranty

- 5 years for wood rot and insect damage on timber sets (termites excluded)
- 12 months on hardware and accessories
- Parts replacement only—no call-outs covered

### Pros

- Widest range from budget to premium
- Many models include slides and platforms as standard
- Available through mainstream retailers (Bunnings, Big W, Myer)
- Showrooms allow in-person viewing
- Professional installation available in metro areas

### Cons

- Brand-level customer service reputation is poor despite decent product reviews
- Timber cracking reported by some customers
- Warranty excludes termite damage
- Third-party delivery can be unreliable (missing boxes, delays)
- Timber needs ongoing maintenance

## Funky Monkey Bars

![](/images/posts/funky-monkey-bars.avif)

[**View at Funky Monkey Bars**](https://www.funkymonkeybars.com/collections/swing-sets)

Funky Monkey Bars is a Perth-based company and the only genuinely Australian-manufactured option on this list. Their frames use Bluescope galvanised steel with Dulux powder coating, designed to Australian playground safety standards.

### What parents should know

**Built for kids and adults.** Load ratings range from 420kg to 1,920kg depending on configuration—far higher than other brands. Adults can comfortably use these for exercise, which adds value over the equipment's lifetime.

**Height adjustable.** Frames adjust from 1.2m to 2.3m (up to 3m with extenders), so the set genuinely grows with your children from toddler through teenager.

**Free-standing design.** No concrete required—frames anchor with screws into grass, making them suitable for renters or families who might move.

**Primarily focused on monkey bars.** Swing sets are part of their modular system, but the brand's strength is monkey bars and ninja-style elements rather than cubby houses and slides.

**Frames don't include swings.** When comparing prices, note that you'll need to add swings separately. Budget accordingly.

### Warranty

- 10 years on frames
- 1 year on accessories
- Half-price replacement swings available after warranty expires

### Cons from user reviews

Some owners report accessories (rubber grips, ninja balls) degrading in sun faster than expected, and isolated complaints about rust on swing chains after 17 months. The 1-year accessory warranty feels short given the premium positioning.

### Pros

- Only Australian-manufactured option
- Industrial-grade durability—designed to AS 4685 playground standard
- Adults can use it for exercise
- Height adjustable for long-term use
- Free-standing design allows relocation
- Strong resale market

### Cons

- Premium pricing—expect to spend significantly more than timber alternatives
- Swings sold separately, so advertised prices can be misleading
- Shipping from Perth means longer delivery times to eastern states
- Limited slide options compared to Plum/Lifespan
- Accessory warranty only 1 year despite premium price

## Which set suits which family?

**If you want a slide included:** Skip Vuly. Look at Plum Play or Lifespan Kids—many of their models include slides, platforms, and climbing elements as standard.

**If you're on a tight budget:** Lifespan Kids' basic metal swing sets are the most affordable option. Plum Play's entry-level wooden sets also offer reasonable value.

**If you prioritise durability and low maintenance:** Vuly Max 2 or Funky Monkey Bars. Steel construction handles Australian conditions without annual timber treatment.

**If you want adults to use it too:** Funky Monkey Bars. Their load ratings (up to 1,920kg) and height adjustability make them suitable for family exercise, not just kids' play.

**If you have a small backyard:** Plum Play's Tamarin has one of the most compact footprints for a full-featured set.

**If you might move house:** Funky Monkey Bars' free-standing design can be relocated without dealing with concrete footings.

**If Australian-made matters to you:** Funky Monkey Bars is the only option manufactured in Australia.

**If you want to see it before buying:** Lifespan Kids has showrooms in Melbourne and Sydney.

## Final thoughts

There's no single "best" swing set—it depends on your budget, backyard size, and what features matter to your family.

For pure swing systems with long-term durability, Vuly Max 2 and Funky Monkey Bars justify their higher prices through steel construction and strong warranties (8 years and 10 years respectively). Funky Monkey Bars edges ahead if you want adults to use the equipment too.

For swing-plus-slide combinations at accessible prices, Plum Play and Lifespan Kids offer more complete packages. Just be prepared for ongoing timber maintenance and check warranty terms carefully—particularly around termite damage and saltwater proximity.

Whatever you choose, factor in assembly time realistically, measure your backyard including fall zones (typically 2m clearance on all sides), and consider whether you want a set that grows with your kids or one that suits their current age.
