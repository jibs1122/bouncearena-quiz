Vuly has recently updated their trampoline line, introducing the Thunder 2 and Thunder 2 Pro models. If you're considering a new trampoline for your family, you might be wondering how these two models compare.

Let's take a closer look at their features, similarities, and differences.

## What's New in the Thunder Series?

Both the Thunder 2 and Thunder 2 Pro introduce several improvements from the original Thunder models:

### Flexible Net Poles

The new models feature more flexible net poles, designed to withstand greater force without damage. This enhancement aims to improve both safety and durability.

### Dual Accessory Use

![](/images/posts/vuly-shade-cover-and-basketball.jpg)

Unlike previous models, these new trampolines allow simultaneous use of the basketball hoop and shade cover.

### Improved Rust Protection

Both models now feature galvanised and powder-coated steel frames for superior rust resistance.

### Stronger Anchor Kit

Vuly has introduced new anchor kits that are reportedly five times stronger than previous versions.

## Thunder 2 vs Thunder 2 Pro: Key Differences

While sharing many features, the Thunder 2 and Thunder 2 Pro do have some notable differences:

### Frame Size

The Thunder 2 Pro has a slightly larger frame width. In the Large size, for example, the Thunder 2 Pro measures 415cm compared to the Thunder 2's 409cm.

### Spring Technology

While both use leaf spring technology, the Thunder 2 Pro features full-length leaf springs, which Vuly claims provides a smoother and safer bounce.

### Underside Access

The Thunder 2 allows underside access, while the Thunder 2 Pro does not. This difference may affect safety considerations for some users.

![](/images/posts/vuly-thunder-2-pro.jpg)

### Size Options

The Thunder 2 is available in four sizes (M, L, XL, XXL), while the Thunder 2 Pro comes in three sizes (M, L, XL).

### Price Point

There's a significant price difference between the two models. The Thunder 2 is currently priced at $1,499 for the Large size, while the Thunder 2 Pro costs $2,499 for the same size\*.

\*Prices valid at time of writing.

## Shared Features

Despite their differences, both the Thunder 2 and Thunder 2 Pro share several key features:

- No external springs (leaf springs are internal)
- Super strong and flexible net poles
- Galvanised and powder-coated steel frame
- 191cm safety net height
- 375cm mat width (for Large size)
- 91cm mat height
- 288cm overall trampoline height
- Weight capacity of 150kg on M and L sizes, 140kg on XL size
- Compatibility with accessories like shade covers, tent walls, and basketball hoops
- Identical warranty terms (10 years for frame, 5 years for springs and mat, 1 year for safety net)

## Choosing Between the Thunder 2 and Thunder 2 Pro

Your decision between these two models will likely depend on your specific needs and budget. Here are some factors to consider:

The Thunder 2 might be a good fit if:

- You're working with a tighter budget
- You want the XXL size option
- The standard leaf spring technology meets your bouncing needs

The Thunder 2 Pro could be worth considering if:

- You're willing to invest more for potentially smoother bounce
- The full-length leaf spring technology appeals to you
- You want no underside access
- You’re happy with the slightly larger frame size

Both trampolines offer significant improvements over previous models and are designed to provide safe, enjoyable use for families. Consider your priorities, available space, and budget to determine which model aligns best with your needs.
