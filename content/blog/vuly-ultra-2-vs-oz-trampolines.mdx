If you're looking to add a high-quality trampoline to your backyard, you've likely come across the Vuly Ultra 2 and the Oz Trampolines OzUltimate in your research. Both are excellent choices that share the same frame design and represent their brands' newer models with advanced safety features.

Oz Trampolines has built its reputation on durable products designed specifically for harsh Australian weather, while Vuly has also established itself as a trusted Australian brand with innovative designs and accessories.

Let's walk through what you need to know to make the right choice for your family.

## The Safety Evolution

Both the Vuly Ultra 2 and the OzUltimate represent significant steps forward in trampoline safety design. The OzUltimate is Oz Trampolines' first model to feature externally positioned springs and curved net poles - design elements that the Vuly Ultra 2 also incorporates.

These safety innovations provide several advantages:

- **External Springs:** By positioning the springs outside the jumping area, both models dramatically reduce the risk of pinch injuries that can occur when jumpers come into contact with springs.
- **Curved Net Poles:** The outward-curving poles create additional distance between jumpers and the frame, minimizing potential impact injuries.
- **Enhanced Enclosure Systems:** Both feature high-quality mesh enclosures with safety nets attached directly to the jumping mat, creating fully contained jumping environments.

## Safety Features

Both trampolines claim to exceed Australian safety standards (AS 4989:2015), with multiple design elements focused on keeping jumpers secure:

- **Entry Systems:** Both models feature the same zipless entry system - the Vuly Ultra 2 has a self-closing entry, while the OzUltimate uses an overlapping tunnel entrance design. We prefer no-zip entries as zips tend to fail over time.
- **No-Contact Frame Design:** Both models are engineered to keep jumpers away from the frame and springs during use.

## Materials Built for Australian Conditions

Both trampolines use galvanized steel frames designed to withstand our harsh Australian climate. The materials are specifically selected to handle everything from intense sun exposure to rain and temperature fluctuations.

It's worth noting that more premium models like the [Vuly Ultra 2 Pro](/go/vuly-ultra-2-pro) add powder coating as an additional layer of protection against rust, which isn't featured on either of these trampolines.

## Price and What's Included

When it comes to what you get for your money, here are the key differences:

**Oz Trampolines OzUltimate ($1,199 RRP **for 12ft size**)**

- Comes with an anchor kit to secure the trampoline
- Includes a 2-step ladder for easy access
- Free delivery anywhere in Australia
- 5-year warranty on the frame, 2-year warranty on other parts

**Vuly Ultra 2 ($899 RRP for Large size)**

- Lower initial price point, but ladder and anchor kit aren't included as standard
- Currently comes with various free bonus items
- Free delivery to most of Australia
- 5-year warranty on frame, springs, and mat; 1-year warranty on safety net and pads

The prices listed are current at the time of writing. Both companies regularly run promotions, so it's worth checking their current offers before making your final decision.

## Accessory Options to Consider

Both brands offer accessories to enhance your trampoline experience, but there are some differences that might matter to your family:

**Vuly Ultra 2 Accessories:**

- Tent walls that transform the trampoline into a play tent or sleepover space
- Shade covers to protect from sun exposure
- Basketball hoops for added play value

**Oz Trampolines OzUltimate Accessories:**

- Trampoline wheels that make it easy to move when you're mowing the lawn or repositioning
- Shade covers similar to Vuly's options
- Basketball hoops for sports play

## Making Your Decision

Since these trampolines are remarkably similar in design and safety features, your choice might come down to:

**The Oz Trampolines OzUltimate might be better if:**

- You want everything included out of the box (anchor kit and ladder)
- You'll need to move your trampoline regularly and value the wheel accessory option
- The specific dimensions work better for your space

**The Vuly Ultra 2 might be better if:**

- You're working with a tighter budget and don't mind purchasing accessories separately
- You're interested in Vuly's current promotional offers
- Your kids would love the tent wall option for play or sleepovers
- You prefer the slightly taller safety net (191 cm vs 180 cm)

While the warranties differ (Vuly offers longer coverage on springs and mat, while Oz provides better coverage on the safety net and pads), this probably shouldn't be your deciding factor.

Ultimately, with trampolines this similar in quality and features, the best deal at the time you're ready to purchase will likely be the smartest choice for your family.
