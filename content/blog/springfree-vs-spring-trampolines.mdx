Trampolines have become a staple in many Australian backyards, offering endless hours of fun and exercise for kids and adults alike.

But when it comes to choosing the right trampoline, parents are faced with an important decision: traditional spring trampolines or the newer springfree models?

This choice isn't just about which one bounces better. It's about safety, durability, cost, and ultimately, which option will provide the best experience for your family.

In this article, we'll dive deep into the world of trampolines, comparing springfree and spring models to help you make an informed decision.

## The Basics: What Sets Them Apart?

Spring trampolines are the old-school champs. They use coiled metal springs to create that classic bouncy feel we all know and love. These springs connect the jumping mat to the frame, creating tension that delivers serious bounce.

![](/images/posts/vuly-ultra-net-and-mat-union.jpg)

[Springless trampolines](/springless-trampolines), on the other hand, are the new kids on the block. They use materials such as flexible composite rods instead of metal springs. These rods are tucked away beneath the jumping surface, out of harm's way.

![](/images/posts/springfree-springs.png)

But the differences don't stop there. Let's break it down further.

## Safety: Jumping Without Worry

When it comes to trampolines, safety is the top priority for most parents. After all, you want your kids to have fun, not end up with bumps and bruises.

### Spring Trampolines

Traditional spring trampolines have come a long way in terms of safety. Many now come with:

- Padded spring covers to prevent pinching
- Safety nets to keep jumpers from falling off
- Reinforced frames for stability
- Springs external to jumping area

![](/images/posts/advanced-safety-features.png)

However, there's still a risk of feet slipping between springs or fingers getting caught. The frame and springs are also typically at the same level as the jumping surface, which can lead to injuries if a jumper lands on them.

### Springless Trampolines

Springless trampolines, like [Springfree and Vuly Thunder models](/vuly-vs-springfree), were designed with safety as the main focus. They offer several advantages:

- No exposed springs, eliminating pinch points and gaps
- Frame positioned below the jumping surface, reducing impact injuries
- Flexible rods or leaf springs tucked away out of reach
- High-quality nets that attach directly to the mat, preventing falls

Our trampoline expert notes that Springfree trampolines are "the safest trampolines in Australian backyards."

## Bounce: Getting Your Money's Worth

The bounce is what makes a trampoline fun. But spring and springless trampolines offer different experiences.

### Spring Trampolines

Spring trampolines are known for their:

- Responsive, powerful bounce
- Familiar feel that many people prefer
- Ability to achieve greater heights (though this can also be a safety concern)

### Springless Trampolines

Springless trampolines provide a different kind of bounce:

- Often described as softer and more controlled
- Consistent performance across different weight ranges

[](/images/posts/vuly-thunder-bounce.mp4)

## Assembly: The Set-Up Showdown

Let's face it, putting together a trampoline can be a test of patience. Here's how spring and springless models compare:

### Spring Trampolines

Generally, spring trampolines are easier to assemble:

- Familiar design that many find intuitive
- Fewer parts overall
- Can often be set up by one or two people in a few hours

However, attaching the springs can be physically demanding and may require special tools.

### Springless Trampolines

Springless trampolines are typically more complex to assemble:

- Innovative designs mean less familiar setup process
- More parts to put together
- Often require at least two people and several hours

![](/images/posts/springfree-trampoline-assembly.png)

## Cost: Investing in Backyard Fun

Price is often a deciding factor when choosing a trampoline. Let's break it down:

### Spring Trampolines

Spring trampolines generally have a lower upfront cost:

- Wide range of prices to suit different budgets
- Can find decent models from $500 to $1500 AUD

### Springless Trampolines

Springless trampolines are typically more expensive:

- Prices often range from $1500 to $2500+ AUD
- Higher upfront cost reflects innovative design and advanced safety features

## The Bounce-Off: Making Your Choice

So, which type of trampoline should you choose? Here's a quick summary to help you decide:

Choose a spring trampoline if:

- You're working with a tighter budget
- You prefer a more traditional, powerful bounce
- You don't mind a slightly higher safety risk

Go for a springless trampoline if:

- Safety is your top priority
- You're willing to invest more upfront

Remember, both types can provide years of fun and exercise for your family. The most important thing is that you choose a trampoline that fits your space, budget, and safety requirements.

## Wrapping It Up: The Final Bounce

Choosing between spring and springless trampolines isn't just about picking a bouncy surface for your kids. It's about investing in years of outdoor fun, exercise, and memories for your whole family.

Spring trampolines offer that classic bouncing experience at a more affordable price, with safety features that have significantly improved over the years. Springless trampolines provide great safety and durability, with a unique bouncing experience that many find more comfortable.

Whichever you choose, make sure to follow the safety guidelines, supervise your kids during use, and most importantly, join in the fun yourself!
