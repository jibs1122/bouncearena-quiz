Choosing a trampoline for your family is an exciting yet daunting task. You want something safe, durable, and of course, fun for everyone.

The Vuly Ultra and Vuly Thunder are two promising options, each with unique features and benefits. If you’ve been puzzling over which one to pick, you're in the right place.

This guide aims to help you navigate the key similarities and differences, so you can make the best decision for your family.

## **Build Quality and Materials**

The **Vuly Ultra** is built to last, featuring a robust galvanised steel frame. Its durable construction is designed to withstand various weather conditions, although it lacks a powder-coated finish that would provide additional protection.

![](/images/posts/vuly-ultra-trampoline-1.png)

The Ultra’s frame, springs, mat, and padding are all strong and long-lasting, making it a solid choice for families.

Here are some highlights:

- **Galvanised Steel Frame**: Strong and durable, but lacks powder coating.
- **70 Springs**: Each 13.7cm in length, offering robust performance.
- **Durability**: Withstands various weather conditions; recommended to secure with an anchor kit for extra stability.

In contrast, the **Vuly Thunder** boasts a unique springless design with leaf springs instead of traditional coils. This design eliminates common safety concerns related to pinching and entrapment.

![](/images/posts/vuly-thunder-springs.jpg)

However, some users have reported that the leaf springs can crack and snap from normal use, which might be a dealbreaker for some. The Thunder’s galvanised steel frame is equally robust, but it also lacks the protective powder coating.

Key points include:

- **Springless Design**: Uses leaf springs, reducing the risk of pinching.
- **Leaf Springs**: Innovative but prone to cracking from regular use.
- **Strong Frame**: Galvanised steel for durability.

## **Safety Features**

Safety is paramount when it comes to trampolines, and both the Ultra and Thunder excel in this area.

### **Vuly Ultra**

- **Meets Australian Safety Standard AS 4989:2015**: Ensures high safety levels.
- **High Safety Net**: 175cm tall, preventing falls.
- **Curved Safety Net Poles**: Reduce the risk of contact with the frame.
- **Robust Mat**: Small hands cannot contact the springs from inside the trampoline.
- **Overlapping Door**: No zips to break.

Our expert notes, “This is a very safe trampoline,” highlighting the tall net and robust mat.

### **Vuly Thunder**

- **Springless Design**: Eliminates risks related to traditional springs.
- **Superfine Mesh Net**: 180cm tall, attaches directly to the mat.
- **Skirt Covering Leaf Springs**: Further reduces the chance of injury.
- **Adheres to Multiple Safety Standards**: Including Australian and European standards (AS 4989:2015, EN71-1:2014, EN71-2:2011+A1, EN 71-14:2014).

“This trampoline is one of the safest on the market,” says our expert.

![](/images/posts/vuly-thunder-1.jpg)

## **Bounce Quality**

Bounce quality is a critical factor for a trampoline's fun factor, and both models deliver, however, the traditional spring design on the Ultra is likely to make it the better option here.

### **Vuly Ultra**

With its 70 springs, the Ultra offers a satisfying bounce for all ages. Its “advanced rebound system” uses two layers of springs to boost momentum based on the bouncer’s weight, making it suitable for everyone, from toddlers to adults.

According to our expert, “This trampoline is great for the smallest kids to the biggest parent and has bounce for everyone.”

Key features include:

- **Advanced Rebound System**: Two layers of springs for better momentum.
- **70 Springs**: Provide a balanced and robust bounce.
- **Versatile Performance**: Suitable for both kids and adults.

### Important

The Ultra doesn't come with an anchor kit. We highly recommend also purchasing the [Vuly Trampoline Anchor Kit](https://www.vulyplay.com/aff/100/?url=accessories/anchor-kit), which will secure your trampoline to the ground.

### **Vuly Thunder**

The Thunder provides a different bounce experience due to its springless design. Larger jumpers will get a better bounce than toddlers, but overall, the bounce is very good.

Highlights include:

- **Leaf Springs**: Offer a unique bounce feel.
- **Great for Larger Jumpers**: Optimised for various weights.
- **Consistent Bounce Quality**: Ensures enjoyable use for all ages.

## **Ease of Assembly**

Assembling a trampoline can be a daunting task, but the Vuly Ultra is generally considered easy to put together.

### **Vuly Ultra**

Some users find the instructions a bit unclear, but Vuly’s assembly videos can help. Our expert suggests that while it’s “very easy to assemble, you do need to know a couple of tricks to get the metal parts to fit together correctly.” He notes that some markers on the mat might be sewn in the wrong place, so pay attention.

![](/images/posts/vuly-ultra-assembly.png)

### **Vuly Thunder**

The assembly process for the Vuly Thunder is more challenging. Users often report difficulties with unclear instructions and the poorly designed leaf spring tool.

“As with all Vuly models, the feedback from customers is the instructions aren’t very helpful and make things harder than should be,” our expert critiques. If you’re not confident in your DIY skills, you might want to hire a professional to set up the Thunder.

## **Value for Money**

When it comes to price, the Vuly Ultra is a significant investment. However, its durable build, extensive safety features, and positive user feedback make it a worthwhile purchase for many families. The comprehensive warranty adds to its appeal, providing peace of mind.

### **Vuly Ultra**

- **Price**: Mid-level
- **Durability**: Strong build and weather resistance.
- **Safety Features**: Extensive and reliable.
- **Warranty**: Competitive, offering 5 years on the frame, springs, and mat, and 1 year on the safety net and pads.

![](/images/posts/vuly-ultra-trampoline-2.png)

### **Vuly Thunder**

The Vuly Thunder, with its premium springless design, is one of the more expensive models on the market. This higher price point reflects the advanced safety features and unique construction.

While it might seem steep, especially considering the assembly challenges and potential durability issues, the Thunder’s innovative design and robust construction justify the cost for families prioritising safety and durability.

- **Price**: Premium
- **Innovative Design**: Unique and advanced.
- **Safety Features**: Superior and comprehensive.
- **Warranty**: Includes 10 years on the frame, 5 years on the jump mat, and 1 year on the safety net, net poles, and leaf springs. An extended warranty is available.

## **Additional Features and Accessories**

Both the Vuly Ultra and Vuly Thunder offer a range of optional accessories, including a shade cover, tent wall, and basketball hoop, which enhance the trampolines’ functionality and enjoyment.

A key difference is that the Vuly Thunder offers wheels for easy movement, whereas the Ultra does not.

Neither trampoline includes a ladder or anchor kit in the basic package, so these must be purchased separately for complete functionality.

## **Conclusion**

Both the Vuly Ultra and Vuly Thunder are excellent trampolines, each with its strengths and weaknesses.

The Ultra stands out for its sturdy build, safety features, and overall value, making it a great choice for most families.

The Thunder, with its innovative springless design and superior safety features, is ideal for those prioritising safety and willing to invest in a premium product.

If you’re looking for a reliable, safe, and fun trampoline that offers good value for money, the Vuly Ultra is a solid choice. It’s easy to assemble, durable, and provides a great bounce for all ages.

However, if safety is your top priority and you’re prepared to handle a more challenging assembly process, the Vuly Thunder is an excellent option. Its springless design and robust construction make it one of the safest trampolines on the market.
